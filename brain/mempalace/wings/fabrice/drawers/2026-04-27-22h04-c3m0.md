**Comment 5 — Ravi Sharma**

[CONTENT:linkedin-comment-05]
Shop Pay lift is real but it's downstream of a bigger problem. By the time a buyer clicks "Checkout", some stores have already lost them — the checkout render itself is slow because of third-party JS injected by apps running in the background.

I've scanned stores where the "Place order" button takes 1.2–1.8s to become interactive after the checkout page loads. Not because of Shop Pay, not because of form fields — purely app bloat firing on a page that should be dead simple.

Shopify Analytics doesn't surface this. You see abandoned checkouts but not why the checkout felt laggy before they even started typing.
[/CONTENT]

**Comment 6 — Falko Saft**

[CONTENT:linkedin-comment-06]
Makes sense at that scale. The math shifts somewhere around $500k–$1M/year — at that point the cumulative cost of checkout apps (monthly fees + performance drag + dev time managing conflicts) starts competing with what it would cost to just own it internally.

The performance drag is the part that's hardest to quantify before you've actually measured it. StoreMD scans show that stores with 15+ apps injecting JS into the checkout flow average 800ms+ of extra latency before the button is even clickable. That's not a conversion optimization problem. That's an architecture problem.
[/CONTENT]
