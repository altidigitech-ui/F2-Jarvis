**Comment 1 — Jenn V.**

[CONTENT:linkedin-comment-01]
Most merchants expect 1-2 surprises when they actually do this exercise. What they find is closer to 5-6, and at least a couple from apps they'd thought were gone months ago.

Shopify treats app installs and billing subscriptions as two separate systems. Uninstalling removes the app. It doesn't touch the charge. The ghost billing keeps running with nothing visible in your dashboard because the app isn't there anymore.

Across 90+ stores I've scanned, that pattern shows up in roughly 85% of them. The amounts feel small individually ($12, $19, $29) but over 6-12 months it compounds fast.
[/CONTENT]

**Comment 2 — Matthew Hapgood**

[CONTENT:linkedin-comment-02]
App installs and billing subscriptions are separate records on Shopify's side. By design, not by accident. Uninstalling kills one. The other keeps running.

What makes it worse: the cancellation step lives inside the app's own settings page. You can only access it before uninstalling, not after. So by the time you realize the charge is still active, the option to clean it up cleanly is already gone.

Across 90+ store scans, average ghost billing window after removal is 4-6 weeks. Some merchants are months in before they catch it.
[/CONTENT]

[CONTENT-FR]
Install et facturation sont deux enregistrements séparés côté Shopify, par design. Désinstaller supprime l'un, pas l'autre. Et l'étape d'annulation n'est accessible qu'avant la désinstallation. Sur 90+ scans, la fenêtre moyenne de ghost billing post-suppression est 4-6 semain
