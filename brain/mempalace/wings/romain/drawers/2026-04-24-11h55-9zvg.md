**Action 1 — @andreiRS** (🔴 PRIORITÉ — "Who is ChatGPT recommending to your customers?")
URL : `https://www.indiehackers.com/post/who-is-chatgpt-recommending-to-your-customers-instead-of-you-i-will-run-this-for-the-first-20-indie-saas-founders-for-free-94d1944b80`

[CONTENT:comment-ih-f2-andreiRS]
We've been running into this from the Shopify side. Merchants wonder why their store shows up in ChatGPT recommendations for their category sometimes and completely disappears for others, with no obvious explanation.

The pattern from 50+ store scans: product pages with proper schema markup get cited 3-4x more consistently across LLMs than stores with the same content in flat text descriptions. Same niche, same review count, same price point. The structured data layer drives most of that difference.

What also surprised us is how differently GPTBot, Claude-Web and PerplexityBot crawl the same store. They don't index the same pages. A store can be highly visible on one engine and essentially invisible on another. We ended up building a per-LLM readiness score into our store health tool because averaging them together was hiding the gap.
[/CONTENT][CONTENT-FR]On voit ça du côté Shopify. Les marchands ne comprennent pas pourquoi leur store apparaît dans les recommandations ChatGPT parfois et disparaît sur d'autres requêtes. Pattern de 50+ scans : les pages avec du schema markup sont citées 3-4x plus souvent. GPTBot, Claude-Web et PerplexityBot n'indexent pas les mêmes pages. On a builder un score de lisibilité par LLM dans StoreMD parce que la moyenne cachait trop l'écart.[/CONTENT-FR]
